# Perspective on E-Corp

Every time someone says E Corp, I hear Evil Corp. Maybe that's my brain trying to simplify the world. Or maybe it's the only honest translation left.

They're not just a company. They're the operating system everyone runs without realizing it. They decide what you're worth, what you owe, what you fear, and somehow convince you it was your choice all along.

They don't need guns or masks. They have contracts, algorithms, debt, and advertisements. They package control so neatly that people mistake it for convenience. They sell comfort while quietly owning the parts of you that matter.

The worst part is they don't force anyone to believe in them. We do it ourselves. Every paycheck, every loan, every tap of a credit card keeps the machine alive. We feed it because we've forgotten what life looks like without it.

People think Evil Corp is the villain. They're wrong. Evil Corp is just the face. The real enemy is the system that lets power hide behind logos and quarterly reports until nobody remembers there are actual people making these decisions.
